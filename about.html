What is Anaphylaxis?

Anaphylaxis is an life threatening emergency that results from an allergic reaction. In anaphylaxis, the body is flooded with chemicals and goes into shock. The airway constricts and blood pressure drops, leaving the person unable to breathe. Anaphylaxis can cause death within 5-30 minutes. It is treatable with a shot of epinephirine as soon as possible.

What are the symptoms?

Some symptoms include:

  • Having recently come in contact with something they are allergic to
  • Swelling, especially around the face area
  • Hives or irritation on the skin
  • Difficulty or inability to breathe
  • Pale or blue skin
  • Weak and rapid pulse
  • Low blood Pressure
  • Dizziness
  • Fainting

If you or anyone you see is suffering from these symptoms, administer an epipen as soon as possible and call 911.
